2 · The forward model (Equation 1)

🟠 MRI A T2-weighted image at echo time \(\text{TE}_i\) becomes measured radial k-space by three operations: weight by coil sensitivities, Fourier-encode on the radial trajectory, sample.

\( y_i = \mathcal{T}_i\, S\, \bar{x}_i + \epsilon_i \)

🟣 terms \(\bar{x}_i\in\mathbb{C}^{N}\) true image · \(S\) coil sensitivities · \(\mathcal{T}_i\) nonuniform FFT (NUFFT, image→k-space) for TE\(_i\)’s radial spokes · \(\epsilon_i\) noise · \(y_i\in\mathbb{C}^{M}\) measured k-space. Few spokes per TE ⇒ \(y_i\) is badly undersampled ⇒ the adjoint image is streaky. That is the problem the network fixes.

3 · The data-consistency step (Equation 9) — the CS→DL bridge

🟢 intuition Each cascade does two things: a CNN denoises the current image guess, then a data-consistency (DC) step drags it back toward the measured k-space with one gradient-descent step of learned size \(\eta\):

\( x''^{(j)} = \hat{x}'^{(j)} - \eta\, A^{*}\!\big(A\hat{x}'^{(j)} - y\big), \qquad \hat{x}'^{(j)} = f_{\text{CNN}}^{(j)}(x'^{(j-1)}) \)

📌 remember Compare to ISTA: \( x^{k+1} = \operatorname{prox}_{\gamma\lambda R}\big(x^k - \gamma A^{H}(Ax^k-y)\big)\). The learned CNN replaces the proximal operator of a hand-designed regularizer, and the learned \(\eta\) replaces the fixed step \(\gamma\). Master this one mapping and you understand the whole architecture.
⚠️ caution The paper’s \(A^{*}\) is not a true adjoint — it folds in density compensation (\(A^{*}:=A^{H}D\)). Keep a real \(A^{H}\) for testing (it passes the dot-product test) separate from the preconditioned \(A^{*}\) used in DC.
